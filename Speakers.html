Speakers

Meet this year's keynote speakers and notable guests.

Andrew Blum

A renowned author and journalist, Blum writes about architecture, design, technology, urbanism, art, and travel. His recent book The Weather Machine explores the intricate networks required to create our weather foracasts.

Janette Sadik-Khan

Janette is an expert on uniting safety, sustainability, and mobility. Former Commissioner of the New York City Department of Transportation, she now works with mayors to redesign cities through innovative projects.

William McDonough

McDonough was first introduced to the closed loop cycle commonly known as cradle-to-cradle while living in Japan. Since then, he has used the model to further sustainable development at a global and molecular scale.

Logan Evasco

Evasco is the Brand and Visual Design Director at True Link Financial. As part of the new wave of climate designers, Logan focuses on regenerative design strategy and community organizing.

Billy Fleming

Fleming focuses on the intersection of science, politics, and design in relation to climate change. His work has taken him from working in urban policy development under the Obama administration through research on nature-based climate adaptation strategies.

Zeynep Tufecki

Tufecki is a Turkish writer, academic, and techno-sociologist. She focuses on artificial inteligence (AI) and big data in the context of politics and corporate social responsbility.

Negar Kalantar

Dr. Kalantar seeks to unite architecture, science, and engineering. She investigates the capabilities of transformable deisgn principles to create adaptive designs, creating spaces that can change for users and the environment.

Jordyn Bonds

Bonds is an experienced UX designer and front-end developer who uses the Internet as a potential to solve human problems. She is the co-founder and Produt Lead for a data diary app called TallyLab.

Masamichi Udagawa and Sigi Moeslinger

Udagawa is the founder of Antenna design and works on user-centered design approach, focusing on the relationship between new technology and mass society. Moesligner is an industrial designer focusing on tactile interactivity to evoke compelling user experiences.
